ROTHSCHILD / The News from Waterloo

{% include rothschild-stories.html %}

  • The News From Waterloo debunks the widely known myth that the first man in London to know of Wellington’s victory at Waterloo was Nathan Rothschild, founder of the bank N.M. Rothschild and Sons, and that Rothschild used this exclusive knowledge to make a killing on the London Stock Exchange.

    Many details are in the book, but there is much more – not least because this myth has adopted many forms over the years and there simply wasn’t room to tackle all of them without giving the whole, half-baked story excessive prominence.

    The pages explore further angles to the myth and look at the evidence or absence of evidence behind them.
